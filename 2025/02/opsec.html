Opsec - What, Why and How

Feb 20, 2025

You’ve probably heard the phrase, “I have nothing to hide” at some point in your life. It’s a common response when people brush off concerns about privacy and security. But let’s be real—this mindset can be pretty risky in our digital age. That’s where Operational Security (OPSEC) comes in. It’s all about keeping your sensitive information safe from prying eyes. Let’s break down what OPSEC is, why it’s important, and how you can get started.

What is OPSEC?

Operational Security, or OPSEC, is simply a fancy way of saying we need to protect our important information. It involves figuring out what you need to keep under wraps, understanding who might want to get their hands on it, and taking steps to keep it secure. OPSEC isn’t just for the military or big corporations; it’s something everyone should think about, especially when it comes to personal privacy and online safety.

Why Should You Care About OPSEC?

Whether it’s your bank details, trade secrets, or personal stuff, losing control of your information can lead to some serious problems. No one wants that!

By knowing the potential threats out there, you can take steps to minimize them. It might mean tightening your online habits or beefing up your security measures.

If you run a business, showing that you care about OPSEC can go a long way in building trust with your customers. When people know their info is secure, they’re more likely to stick around.

How to Get Started with OPSEC

Take a good look at your current practices. Where could you be exposed? This could be anything from weak passwords to sharing too much online. Identifying these vulnerabilities is key to strengthening your defenses. I believe that the two key goals for maintaining effective OPSEC as a regular internet user are to limit information sharing and to utilize secure technology.

Limit Information Sharing

Start by adopting a need-to-know mindset when you’re posting or sharing online. Before you share something personal on social media, ask yourself if it’s really necessary. For example, while it might seem harmless to post about your upcoming vacation, that information can signal to others that your home will be empty. Think about who will see your posts and whether that’s who you want knowing your personal business.

Additionally, you can search for yourself online to see what information is publicly available. If you find any old posts or accounts that you no longer want out there, take the time to delete or update them. Also consider using a data removal service; there are countless online that will send requests on your behalf to have your personal information taken down from various websites and data brokers. Use Secure Technology

Secure Messaging

When it comes to keeping your chats private, secure messaging apps are essential. Think of them as your digital fortresses, ensuring that your conversations stay confidential and are only visible to you and the people you trust.

Signal and SimpleX are some solid alternatives to WhatsApp if you’re willing to sacrifice some convenience for enhanced privacy and security.

Virtual Private Networks (VPNs)

Next on the list is using a VPN. This nifty tool encrypts your internet connection and hides your IP address, especially useful when you’re on public Wi-Fi. So, if you’re chilling at a café and want to browse without worrying about hackers, a VPN is your best friend.

Mullvad is a personal favorite of mine! Their servers never store any activity logs of any kind and payments can be done via cryptocurrencies like Monero.

Secure Password Managers

Let’s face it: keeping track of passwords can be a nightmare. That’s where password managers like KeePassXC come in. They store your passwords securely and even generate strong, unique ones for each of your accounts. Plus, many of them offer two-factor authentication (2FA), adding an extra layer of protection. It’s like having a personal security guard for your passwords!

Regular Software Updates

Don’t overlook the importance of keeping your software updated! Updates often fix security vulnerabilities that could be exploited by hackers. So, make it a habit to check for updates on your operating system and apps regularly to stay ahead of the game.

Identity Theft Protection Services

Identity theft protection services monitor your personal information online and let you know if they find it in places where it shouldn’t be—like the dark web. If something does go wrong, these services can help you recover your identity and guide you on how to keep your data safe.

Remember, OPSEC isn’t a one-and-done deal. Regularly check in on your practices and stay updated on new threats. Doing routine checks and drills can help everyone remember their role in keeping things secure.
